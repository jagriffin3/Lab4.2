About

Ever since childhood I have wanted to be an archaeologist, and I am proud to have achieved that goal. After serving my country through two tours in Iraq with the United States Army, I followed my aspiration by beginning the University of Wisconsin-La Crosse archaeology program in the fall of 2009, earning my Bachelor of Science degree in the Spring of 2013. During my final year at UW-La Crosse, I was accepted to the University of Southampton, Southampton England, for an accelerated masterâ€™s program in Maritime Archaeology, where I was privileged to be able to learn from two of my archaeology heroes, Doctor Jon Adams and Doctor Lucy Blue.

GIS has been an invaluable tool in the field of archaeology and to me, personally, as I have moved forward in my profession. While I have a basic understanding of GIS, I believe that greater knowledge of these tools will be essential to furthering my career, equipping me to create GIS maps and to analyze archaeological data for future cultural resource papers that will help to protect our cultural heritage. Mastering these skills will also be beneficial to my later career in that I will be qualified to be placed in positions where I will be leading excavations and, eventually, heading a cultural resource management company..

In the fall of 2020 I attended the University of Wisconsin-Madison to learn more about GIS and chartography and earn a Masters of Science degree in Cartography/GIS. I have learned a lot with my time at UW-Madison and I hope to apply this to my future.
